Data Cleaning and Preparation

The initial dataset consisted of two separate files: one containing fund performance metrics and another with fund profiles. To prepare the data for analysis, we performed a series of cleaning and transformation steps to create a unified, analysis-ready dataset.

1. Data Loading and Initial Assessment

We began by loading two CSV files into pandas DataFrames:

  • Fund Performance: Contained 83 columns with metrics like monthly returns, Sharpe ratio, and assets under management (AuM).
  • Fund Profiles: Included 61 columns of descriptive information such as fund name, manager name, inception date, and investment strategy.

2. Feature Selection

To create a focused and manageable dataset, we selected a subset of relevant columns from each file. This step was crucial to remove redundant or unnecessary data.

3. Merging Datasets

The two DataFrames were merged using an inner join on 'Fund ID'. This ensured that the resulting dataset only contained funds for which we had both profile and performance information, creating a complete record for each entry.

4. Data Transformation and Type Conversion

We performed several transformations to standardize the data types. The 'Inception Date' was converted to a proper datetime format. All return-related columns, which were originally strings with percentage signs and commas, were converted to numeric types to allow for calculations. Categorical fields were also appropriately typed to improve memory efficiency.

5. Handling Missing Values

Finally, we removed any rows that contained missing values (NaNs) or Other/ - . This step was critical to ensure the quality and reliability of our analysis, leaving us with a clean and complete dataset of 2,059 funds and 29 columns.

This paper by Timothy Krause showing that hedge fund returns are linked to market uncertainty using the VVIX index (volatility) as a simple proxy. Measuring Risk in the Hedge Fund Sector By: Tobias Adrian

This paper shows that hedge fund returns are closely tied to volatility and to covariances across funds, and compares correlations/volatilities around the 1998 Long Term Capital Management LP crisis with equity-market behavior before the crisis. The takeaway is that sector-wide co-movement and volatility are key to understanding hedge fund risk.
