Summary of Findings

Across strategies, we observe clear differences in risk, return, and overall efficiency. Relative Value and Multi-Strategy funds tend to deliver stronger risk-adjusted performance, while Macro and Equity Long/Short exhibit wider variability across metrics. Temporal patterns show that many strategies move together during certain market conditions, suggesting shared exposures to broader macro drivers.

We also find only a weak relationship between fund size and performance, indicating that larger AUM does not guarantee better results. Taken together, the cross-metric comparisons highlight that no single strategy consistently outperforms on every dimension, reinforcing the importance of evaluating multiple factors and maintaining diversification across approaches.

Key Takeaways

Strategy Performance Varies Significantly

Different hedge fund strategies exhibit distinct risk-return profiles. Relative Value and Multi-Strategy approaches demonstrate superior risk-adjusted returns, while Macro and Equity Long/Short strategies show greater performance dispersion.

Market Conditions Drive Correlation

Many strategies exhibit correlated performance during specific market periods, revealing shared exposures to broader macroeconomic factors. This suggests that diversification across strategies may be less effective during market stress periods.

Size Does Not Guarantee Performance

Our analysis reveals only a weak relationship between fund size (AUM) and risk-adjusted performance. Larger funds do not systematically outperform smaller ones, indicating that scale alone is not a reliable predictor of investment success.

Multi-Dimensional Evaluation is Essential

No single strategy dominates across all performance metrics. Strengths in one dimension often come with tradeoffs in others, emphasizing the need for comprehensive evaluation across returns, volatility, and risk-adjusted measures.

Future Work

Future extensions could incorporate longer time horizons, factor exposures (e.g., equity beta, rates, credit), and rolling-period metrics to better understand regime sensitivity. Additional interactivity—such as filtering by region or fund size—could help investors explore subsets of the universe.

With more detailed data, we could also examine persistence, survivorship bias, and whether top-performing strategies maintain their edge over time. These enhancements would provide deeper insights into the sustainability of strategy performance and help investors make more informed allocation decisions.

Implications for Investors

Investment Strategy Recommendations

Based on our analysis, investors should consider the following when constructing hedge fund portfolios:

  • Diversify Across Strategies - No single strategy excels in all market conditions; a balanced approach reduces concentration risk
  • Prioritize Risk-Adjusted Returns - Focus on Sharpe Ratios and other efficiency metrics rather than raw returns alone
  • Consider Strategy Correlations - Be aware that strategies may converge during market stress, reducing diversification benefits
  • Look Beyond Fund Size - Evaluate funds based on performance metrics rather than AUM as a proxy for quality
